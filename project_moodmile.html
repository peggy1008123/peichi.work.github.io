"Moodmile: A gamified wellness app that helps users balance emotions through movement."
OVERVIEW

Moodmile is a gamified wellness app designed to help users manage negative emotions through physical activity and interactive storytelling. Guided by animal companions, players complete daily movement-based tasks to defeat emotion-based monsters, reinforcing motivation and emotional balance.

MOTIVATION

Many people struggle with maintaining exercise habits, especially when facing stress, anxiety, or burnout. Traditional fitness apps often lack emotional engagement, making it harder for users to stay committed. Moodmile integrates gamification, character-driven storytelling, and personalized goal-setting to transform exercise into a rewarding and immersive experience, encouraging long-term habit formation.

Animal Companions as Emotional Guides
Players are not alone on this journey—friendly animal companions provide encouragement, assign missions, and celebrate achievements. Each companion represents different strengths, guiding users through physical challenges while offering emotional support to maintain motivation.
Personalized Goal-Setting
Each user has unique needs, so Moodmile adapts fitness goals based on personal activity levels. Through a simple onboarding process, the app analyzes user habits and abilities, offering customized challenges that ensure progress at a comfortable pace.
Gamified Experience
Moodmile transforms exercise into an engaging adventure, where users complete missions, earn rewards, and battle emotion-based monsters.

By integrating interactive storytelling and progress-based mechanics, we make staying active fun and rewarding.
RESEARCH
Interview

To design an effective gamified wellness experience, we conducted research to explore the exercise habits, motivations, and challenges faced by young adults with mental health concerns. These insights guided the development of our game to encourage sustained physical activity and mental well-being.

Methodology

We conducted interviews with MSU students (ages 18-24, 2 women, 3 men) , randomly selected from West and IM Circle, two campus recreation centers where students actively engage in exercise. This setting ensured that participants had relevant experience with fitness habits and challenges.

FINDINGS
Habits: Participants exercise between rarely to 4 times a week, with only two of them tracking their activities using basic methods like steps or heart rate monitors.
Emotion Management: Common methods include listening to exercise, music, gaming, and socializing.
Preferences: Participants showed a preference for rewarding gamification, Challenges, and Story Lines.
Health-Related App Experience: Participants used apps like Nike Run Club, and Strava, primarily for tracking and motivation. Key motivations included mood improvement, stress management, and routine building.
INSIGHTS
1. There is a link between regular exercise and improved management of negative emotions among participants.
2. Gamification elements that offer immediate rewards, engaging storylines, and clear progression (e.g., levels, badges) are most appealing to the target audience.
3. Simplifying and integrating tracking features (e.g., steps, heart rate) into the game can provide seamless user experience and encourage consistent use.
– COMPETITOR ANALYSIS –
COMPETITOR SUMMARY
The target audience differs somewhat from that of competitors, but the age distribution is quite broad. Most people prioritize privacy and activity quality, so users tend to desire the ability to choose their interactions freely and selectively. They might not prefer social activities that are overly open for random participation.

In this regard, it might be worth rethinking how to provide users with space in their social interactions, ensuring that functionalities cater to the specific needs of the target audience.

DESIGN
Defining the Design Philosophy
Design guideline

Moodmile's overall design features a vibrant nature-inspired color palette, including sky blue, earth brown, grass green, stone gray, and sand beige. The colors are not overly saturated but still convey a lively and energetic feel.

The logo design is smooth, cute, and visually striking. For typography, the playful and whimsical Slackey font is used for main titles, while the clean and structured Urbanist font is used for detailed content to ensure readability.
UI Design
One of my main responsibilities was designing UI elements, incorporating wood, stone, and vine elements to create Moodmile’s island-themed interface. The overall design maintains a smooth and playful aesthetic, with dialogue boxes and notification frames inspired by natural elements where the animals reside.
Story Intro Animation
As negative emotions disrupt balance, animal companions step up to help humans restore harmony through movement. This animation immerses players in Moodmile’s world with gentle visuals and engaging storytelling.
Prototype
The prototype showcases the core gameplay, user flow, and interactive elements designed to encourage movement and emotional balance. RESULT
CONCLUSION
This project was a collaborative graduation project between two individuals, and it was my first attempt at creating a complete project. I was responsible for all aspects of the UI/UX design. It involved ideation, gathering and researching user needs, and producing the entire design from sketches. I implemented a prototype of the app using SwiftUI, which marked my initial experience with Swift programming. Throughout the process, there were numerous iterations and learning experiences. I encountered various shortcomings and gained valuable insights.

During the initial stages, I had numerous features in mind, but this led to a diluted focus on the most prominent aspects. Lack of familiarity with design thinking caused me to overlook vital factors and details. I'm still learning to conduct effective user research and testing, understanding the varied user needs, and addressing their pain points accurately is a critical area for improvement.

The UI underwent several revisions, and I learned how color combinations and their application impact the overall layers and user experience. I gathered numerous UX insights through this project, prompting me to delve deeper into the concepts of UI/UX.
